### Day 1 – Checklist & Outcomes (Advanced)

Only tick these when you can genuinely do them without reading.

---

#### Concepts & Mental Models

- [ ] I can explain the difference between:
  - Worker, Position, Job Profile, Supervisory Org, Location.
- [ ] I can draw (from memory) a small diagram showing how those objects connect.
- [ ] I understand how a change in:
  - [ ] Supervisory Org  
  - [ ] Position / Job  
  - [ ] Location  
  might impact **approvals, costs, and reporting**.
- [ ] I know what a **Job Change** and a **Delegation** are and why they exist.

---

#### Scenario Thinking

- [ ] I can describe how I would:
  - Investigate a **stuck Job Change** by looking at BP history.
  - Investigate **wrong cost center** on a worker’s expenses by checking position + recent job changes.
  - Design an appropriate **delegation** plan for a manager going on leave.
- [ ] I can think through an **org restructure** and list:
  - Which new supervisory orgs to create.
  - Which positions/workers move.
  - What reports and approvals might break if we don’t plan correctly.

---

#### Communication & Explanation

- [ ] I can explain to a non‑technical person:
  - Why changing a worker’s org/position is more than just “updating a field”.
- [ ] I can explain to IT:
  - How a mistake in Job Change or org setup can lead to **integration or data issues**.

---

#### End-of-Day Outcome Statement

By the end of Day 1, I should be able to honestly say:

- [ ] “I can think through people‑structure issues (orgs, positions, job changes, delegations) like a Workday functional person, not just a beginner.”
- [ ] “I know what I’d check and who I’d talk to if there’s a problem with approvals or costs after a job or org change.”
- [ ] “I feel ready to move into Day 2, where financial processes (P2P/O2C/Record to Report) will build on this worker/org foundation.”

If not, loop through Day 1 scenarios and the quiz again until these feel natural.
