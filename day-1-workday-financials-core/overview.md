### Day 1 – Workday Financials Core & Navigation (Super Deep Dive)

**Big goal for Day 1**: Move from “I know the concepts” (Day 0) to “I can think like a Workday functional consultant who lives in the system every day” for:

- **Navigation & UX** (how real users actually move through Workday).
- **Supervisory Orgs, Locations, Positions** (structure of who reports to whom and where).
- **Job Changes & Delegations** (people movement and backup approvals).
- **Business Process routing & approvals** (how work flows and how that hits finance and controls).

You should come out of Day 1 feeling like you’ve **shadowed a senior Workday functional person** for a week.

---

#### Files Inside Day 1

- `overview.md` (this file): Day 1 roadmap.
- `theory.md`: deep theory + diagrams on:
  - Workday object model around workers and orgs.
  - How org/position/loc changes affect finance, approvals, and reporting.
- `practical-exercises.md`: drills and **scenario + answer** pairs, with:
  - One **theoretical** framing (conceptual).
  - One **practical** framing (what you click / check / say).
- `resources.md`: guidance on what to watch/read if you want **real UI exposure**.
- `checklist-and-outcomes.md`: advanced checklist so you only mark Day 1 “done” if you truly think like a near‑senior here.
- New extra files:
  - `qa-and-scenarios.md`: large Q&A bank for Day 1 topics (orgs, job changes, delegations, BPs).
  - `self-assessment-quiz.md`: quiz focused only on Day 1 material.

---

#### How to Study Day 1 for Maximum Impact

1. **Read theory slowly**
   - Go through `theory.md` section by section.
   - After each section, pause and:
     - Sketch the diagram in your own notebook.
     - Write how this **impacts finance and approvals**.
2. **Do scenario drills**
   - Open `practical-exercises.md` and `qa-and-scenarios.md`.
   - For each scenario:
     - First answer off the top of your head.
     - Then compare to the model answers.
     - Add your own extra variations.
3. **Imagine real screens**
   - Even without a tenant, use the descriptions and:
     - Visualize where things would be (Search, Related Actions, BP history).
     - If you have access to a sandbox/demo, try to validate each flow.
4. **Self-test**
   - Use `self-assessment-quiz.md` at the end of the day.
   - If you struggle, loop back to specific sections.

---

#### Outcomes by End of Day 1 (Upgraded)

By the time you finish all Day 1 material, you should be able to:

- **Explain the worker/org model clearly**:
  - What are supervisory orgs, locations, positions, job profiles, and how they connect.
- **Tell strong stories of impact**:
  - E.g. how a wrong org/position/loc can break approvals, cause reporting issues, or impact compliance.
- **Think through Job Changes and Delegations like a pro**:
  - When they’re needed, what they affect, what can go wrong, and how to investigate.
- **Walk through a business process flow in your head**:
  - Initiation → routing → approvals → notifications → completion, and what data moves where.
- **Sound confident in interviews** on Day‑1 topics:
  - Not just “I know the definitions,” but “Here’s how I would handle this real‑world org/job‑change/delegation issue step‑by‑step.”

If you don’t feel that yet, stay on Day 1 longer and keep drilling scenarios until it feels natural.
