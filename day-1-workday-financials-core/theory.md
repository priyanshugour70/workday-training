### Day 1 – Theory: Workday Core Concepts

#### 1. Workday Object Model (High Level)

- **Supervisory Organizations**
  - Represent reporting hierarchies.
  - Tie workers to managers and drive many approvals.
- **Locations**
  - Physical or virtual places where workers are assigned.
  - Often used in security, reporting, and financial dimensions.
- **Positions vs Job Profiles**
  - Positions = specific “seat” in the org.
  - Job profiles = type of job (e.g. Accountant, Analyst).

#### 2. Worker‑Related Processes Relevant to Financial Systems

- **Job Change**
  - Why it matters for finance: changes in cost centers, companies, or locations affect **financial reporting**.
- **Delegations**
  - Allow approvals and tasks to be routed to another user temporarily.
  - Important for ensuring **business process continuity** and **SOX/compliance**.

#### 3. Business Processes (BP) Overview

- A **Business Process** in Workday is:
  - A chain of steps (e.g. initiate → approve → review → complete).
  - Configured with **routing, conditions, notifications, and integrations**.
- For this JD, pay attention to:
  - **Approvals**, **corrections**, and **re-routing**.
  - Impact on **data quality** and **financial controls**.
