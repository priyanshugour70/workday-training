### Day 2 – Theory: Financial Processes in Workday

#### 1. Core Finance Process Flows

- **P2P (Procure to Pay)** – Requisition → Purchase Order → Receipt → Supplier Invoice → Payment.
- **O2C (Order to Cash)** – Customer Order → Invoice → Cash Receipt.
- **Record to Report** – Journal entries → GL → Trial Balance → Financial Statements.

In Workday, these processes are implemented via **business processes**, **accounting rules**, and **configurable objects**.

#### 2. Spend Categories & General Ledger Accounts

- **Spend Categories**
  - Used to classify spend in procurement and expenses.
  - Map to **GL accounts** via accounting rules.
- **General Ledger Accounts**
  - Part of the **Chart of Accounts**.
  - Represent accounting buckets (e.g. “Office Supplies Expense”, “Cash”).

For this JD, you must understand **creating spend categories and GL accounts** at a conceptual level and what impact they have.

#### 3. Accounting Center (Conceptual)

- **Purpose**: Convert operational events (e.g. HR, supply chain) into **accounting entries**.
- Role in Controllership:
  - Ensures **consistent, rules‑driven accounting**.
  - Supports **data integrity** for financial reporting.

#### 4. Business Process Approvals, Corrections, Re‑Routing

- **Approvals**
  - Ensure appropriate **control and sign‑off** before financial impact.
- **Corrections**
  - Adjust or fix transactions that were entered incorrectly.
- **Re‑routing**
  - Send steps to a different approver (e.g. manager change, delegation).

These are critical for **data integrity**, **auditability**, and **SOX compliance**.
